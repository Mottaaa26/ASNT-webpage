Formulas

\( t_{min} = \frac{2.6 (H - 1) D G}{S E} \)


tmin = The minimun acceptable thickness, in inches, for each course as calculated from the above formula; however, tmin shall not be less than 0.1 inch for any tank course.


D = nominal diameter of tank.

H = Height from the bottom of the shell course under consideration to the maximun liquid level when evaluating and entire shell course, in ft; or height, from the bottom of the length L from the lowest point of the bottom of L of the locally thinned area to the maximun liquid level, in ft; or height from the lowest point within any location of interest to the maximmun liquid level, in ft.


G = Highest specific gravity of the contents.

E = The efficieny of the joint.

S = Maximun allowable stress in pounds per square inch; allowable shell stresses are shown in Table 4-1 for materials listed in the current and previous editions of API Std. 12C and Std. 650. Note: For reconstructed tanks, S shall be per the current applicable standard.
